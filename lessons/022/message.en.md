🇪🇸 *Lesson 22*

Ever wonder why Spanish has TWO different words for "you"? One fits before the verb, one after\. Today you'll finally know which goes where — and you'll unlock a pattern that works for *every* pronoun\.

Here's the secret: Spanish has two teams of pronouns, and they never mix\.

*Team 1 \(before verbs\):* yo, tú, él, ella, nosotros, nosotras, ellos, ellas, usted, ustedes
*Team 2 \(after verbs, after prepositions\):* me, te, lo, la, nos, las, los

Think of it like English "he" vs\. "him"\. You say "he sees" but "I see him"\. Spanish does this for ALL pronouns — even "you"\.

— ¿Dónde está María?
— *La* veo\. — I see her\.
— ¿Y tú?
— *Te* veo también\. — I see you too\.

Now here are 8 examples so this sticks:

_Lo veo_ — I see him / I see it
_Te veo_ — I see you \(informal\)
_La veo_ — I see her / I see it \(feminine\)
_Nos ve_ — He sees us
_Los veo_ — I see them \(masculine/mixed\)
_Las veo_ — I see them \(feminine\)
_Me ves_ — You see me
_Lo ves_ — You see him / You see it

💡 Quick trick: If you can replace it with "him" or "her" in English, use Team 2 in Spanish\. If you can replace it with "he" or "she," use Team 1\.

👉 *Say this out loud right now:* "Ella me ve\. Yo la veo\." \(She sees me\. I see her\.\)
