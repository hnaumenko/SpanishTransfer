🇪🇸 *Lesson 24: The verb that unlocks half your conversations*

*Dar* — to give\. It's one of the top 5 most\-used verbs in Spanish\. Why? Because it's not just about handing someone a gift\. It appears everywhere\.

Here's how it works:
*Doy* — I give
*Das* — you give \(informal\)
*Da* — he/she gives, you give \(formal\)
*Damos* — we give
*Dan* — they give, you guys give

Now here's where it gets real\. Spanish stacks pronouns like LEGO blocks\. *Me* \(me\), *te* \(you\), *lo* \(it\), *la* \(it feminine\), *nos* \(us\)\.

— Are you giving it to me tomorrow?
— *¿Me lo da mañana?*

Notice: *me* \(to me\) comes first, *lo* \(it\) second\.

*Try these out loud:*

_Me da algo_ — He gives me something
_Te lo doy mañana_ — I give it to you tomorrow
_Nos dan tiempo_ — They give us time
_¿Me lo das?_ — Are you giving it to me?
_Ella me da razón_ — She gives me reason
_No me da miedo_ — It doesn't give me fear \(I'm not scared\)
_¿Nos lo dan gratis?_ — Do they give it to us for free?
_Quiero dártelo_ — I want to give it to you

💡 In Spanish, you don't always need future tense\. *Te lo doy mañana* \= I'll give it to you tomorrow\.
