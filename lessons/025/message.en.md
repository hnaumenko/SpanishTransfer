🇪🇸 *Lesson 25*

You've been translating "to" and "for" into Spanish\. Stop\. You don't need them\.

In Spanish, *me* already means "me," "to me," AND "for me\." No extra words needed\. Same with *te* \(you\), *nos* \(us\)\. English keeps adding prepositions\. Spanish? Just uses one word\.

Someone asking *¿Me compras algo?* could mean "Will you buy something FOR me?" or even "from me" \(like a street vendor\)\. Context tells you which\.

Look how simple this gets:

— *¿Puedes cocinar algo?*
— *Sí\. ¿Te lo cocino?*
— Can you cook something?
— Yes\. Shall I cook it for you?

No "for\." No "to\." Just flow\.

*Me habla* — He speaks to me
*Te compro* — I buy for you
*¿Me lo haces?* — Will you do it for me?
*Nos cocina* — She cooks for us
*Me quedo* — I stay \(I remain myself\)
*¿Te quedas?* — Are you staying?
*Nos quedamos hasta las 10* — We're staying until 10:00
*Me lo compra* — He buys it for me

💡 *Me quedo* literally means "I remain myself," but it's how you say "I stay\." Spanish loves reflexive verbs where English doesn't\.

👉 Say this out loud right now: *Me quedo aquí hasta las nueve\.* \(I'm staying here until 9:00\.\)
