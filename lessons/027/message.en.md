🇪🇸 *Lesson 27: Where Adjectives Live in Spanish*

Here's something wild: in Spanish, you don't say "the big car\." You say "the car big\."

Adjectives come *after* the noun, not before\. It feels backwards at first, but it's actually the natural order in Spanish\.

*— ¿Dónde está?
— En el coche rojo\.*
\(Where is it? In the red car\.\)

The pattern is simple:

**NOUN \+ ADJECTIVE**

*la cámara digital* — the digital camera
*el auto grande* — the big car
*la situación global* — the global situation
*una máquina roja* — a red machine
*los coches grandes* — the big cars
*el coche rojo* — the red car
*las casas grandes* — the big houses
*la cámara nueva* — the new camera

💡 When adjectives go plural, add \-s or \-es just like nouns: *grande* → *grandes*\.
