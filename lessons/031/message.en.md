🇪🇸 *Lesson 31: The E That Spanish Can't Live Without*

Ever notice Spanish loves adding an E to the start of words? That's because Spanish *physically can't* start a word with ST\-, SP\-, or SC\-\. English does it all the time\. Spanish? Nope\.

Watch what happens:

*Student* → _estudiante_
*Spain* → _España_
*Special* → _especial_
*Strict* → _estricto_
*Spectacular* → _espectacular_
*School* → _escuela_
*State* → _estado_

And guess what? This is why the verb *to be* looks so different in Spanish\. We have *two* verbs: **estar** \(for states, temporary things\) and **ser** \(for characteristics, who you are\)\.

*Estar* literally comes from the word *state*\. See it now?

Here are 8 examples using *ser* — the verb for characteristics:

_Soy estudiante_ — I'm a student
_Es posible_ — It's possible
_Eres especial_ — You're special
_Es estricto_ — He's strict
_Son de España_ — They're from Spain
_Somos humanos_ — We're human
_Es espectacular_ — It's spectacular
_¿Cómo es?_ — What's he/she like?

💡 Pro tip: When someone asks *¿Cómo estás?* they want to know how you're *doing*\. When they ask *¿Cómo eres?* they want to know what you're *like* as a person\.

👉 Say this out loud right now: *Soy estudiante\. Es especial\.*
