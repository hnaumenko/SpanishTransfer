🇪🇸 *Lesson 36: The Future That's Been Hiding in Plain Sight*

You already know how to say the future tense in Spanish\. You just don't realize it yet\.

Remember *he*, *ha*, *hemos*, *han*? Those endings from "I have eaten," "she has gone"? Drop the H, slap them onto any verb, and boom — you've got the future\.

Take *comer* \(to eat\)\. Add *é*: *comeré* — I will eat\. Add *á*: *comerá* — he will eat\. You're literally pushing the accent into the future\.

Here's the magic: it works with *any* verb\.

*comeré* — I will eat  
*iremos* — we will go  
*dormirá* — he will sleep  
*organizarán* — they will organize  
*prepararé* — I will prepare  
*encontrarás* — you will find  
*esperaré* — I will wait  
*quedaré* — I will stay

Notice something? Even irregular verbs like *ir* become regular in the future: *iré*, *irás*, *irá*\.

💡 One tip: Think the *whole* verb first, then add the ending\. Don't say *"preparé"* — say *"prepararé"\.*

*— ¿Irás a la fiesta?* — Will you go to the party?  
*— No sé\. Estaré cansado\.* — I don't know\. I'll be tired\.

👉 *Say this now:* Pick any verb you know and make it future\. Out loud\.
