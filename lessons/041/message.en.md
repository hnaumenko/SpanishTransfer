🇪🇸 *Lesson 41: The Future Lives in the Past*

You already know how to build the future in Spanish\. You just need to look backwards\.

Here's the secret: that super irregular verb *haber* \(to have\) that you used for the past? It's hiding your future tense inside it\.

Remember *haber*?
_he_ — I have
_has_ — you have
_ha_ — he/she/you formal have
_hemos_ — we have
_han_ — they/you guys have

Now just stick those sounds onto ANY verb, and boom — instant future:

— When's the party?
— Tomorrow\. *Bailaré* mucho\.
\(I'll dance a lot\.\)

Here are 8 you'll actually use:

_bailaré_ — I will dance
_comerás_ — you will eat
_irá_ — he/she/you will go
_hablaremos_ — we will speak
_trabajarán_ — they will work
_visitaré_ — I will visit
_aprenderás_ — you will learn
_vivirá_ — he/she will live

And *would*? Even easier\. Add *\-ía* to any verb:

_bailaría_ — I/he/she/you formal would dance
_iríamos_ — we would go
_comerían_ — they would eat
_hablaría_ — I would speak
_trabajarías_ — you would work

💡 Both future tenses push the accent forward onto the ending\. We're literally stressing the *future* part\.

👉 *Say this now:* "Bailaría pero no tengo tiempo" \(I would dance but I don't have time\)\.
