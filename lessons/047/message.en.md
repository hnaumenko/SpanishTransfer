🇪🇸 *Lesson 47*

Ever noticed Spanish says "the dinner was burning itself" when you just mean "the dinner was burning"? Here's why — and how to use this pattern like a native\.

When something *happens to itself* \(not to something else\), Spanish adds *se*:

— What happened to dinner?
— *La cena se quemaba\.* The dinner was burning\. \(literally: burning itself\)

You can't just say *"quemaba la cena"* — that feels incomplete, like we're waiting to hear *what* was being burned\. But *"la cena se quemaba"* is clear: the dinner itself was getting burned\.

Here are 8 examples where this *se* pattern shows up:

_La cena se quemaba_ — The dinner was burning
_Me quemo en el sol_ — I burn in the sun \(I get burned\)
_Se rompe todo el tiempo_ — It breaks all the time
_Esos se rompen_ — Those ones break
_Se cambia_ — It changes
_El coche se para_ — The car stops
_La puerta se cierra_ — The door closes
_Todo se olvida_ — Everything gets forgotten

💡 The position is flexible: you can say *la cena se quemaba* OR *se quemaba la cena*\. Both work\.
