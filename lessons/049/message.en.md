🇪🇸 *Lesson 49: Three Rebels in the Smoothest Spanish Tense*

The imperfect past is the safest, most regular tense in Spanish\. Almost every verb follows the pattern perfectly\. Except three tiny troublemakers: *ser*, *ver*, and *ir*\.

Here's how they break the rules:

*ser* \(to be\) → *era* — I was / I used to be
Think "an era in time" and you'll never forget it\.

*ver* \(to see\) → *veía* — I was seeing / I used to see
Just like the present *veo*, it keeps that extra *e*\.

*ir* \(to go\) → *iba* — I was going / I used to go
This one's gold\. You can use it like "I was gonna…"

— *Iba a llamarte* — I was going to call you
— *Íbamos a hacerlo pero se nos olvidó* — We were going to do it but we forgot

Now here are eight examples with these rebels:

_Era estudiante_ — I used to be a student
_¿Eras tú estudiante?_ — Were you a student?
_Éramos estudiantes_ — We were students
_Veía películas todos los días_ — I used to watch movies every day
_¿Qué veían ellos?_ — What were they watching?
_Iba al gimnasio cada mañana_ — I used to go to the gym every morning
_Íbamos a viajar pero no teníamos tiempo_ — We were going to travel but didn't have time
_Ellos iban a la playa los domingos_ — They used to go to the beach on Sundays

💡 *Iba a \+ infinitive* is your new best friend for "I was gonna\.\.\."
