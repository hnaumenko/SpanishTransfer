🇪🇸 *Lesson 52*

Ever notice how Spanish speakers learning English say "I'm here *for* see you"? That's not bad English — it's a gift\. They're teaching you how Spanish works\.

In Spanish, whenever "to" means "*in order to*," you need *para*\. You can't just use the verb alone\.

Think about it:
— Why did you call?
— *Te he llamado para hablar de ello\.* \(I called you *in order to* talk about it\.\)

You need that *para* — without it, the sentence breaks\. English lets you skip "in order to," but Spanish doesn't\.

Here are 8 ways to use it:

_Estoy aquí para verte_ — I'm here to see you
_Hemos hablado para resolverlo_ — We've spoken to resolve it
_Te he llamado para hablar de ello_ — I've called you to talk about it
_Estudio para aprender_ — I study to learn
_Trabajo para vivir_ — I work to live
_Vine para ayudarte_ — I came to help you
_Leo para mejorar mi español_ — I read to improve my Spanish
_Salí para comprar café_ — I went out to buy coffee

💡 When you hear a Spanish speaker say something like "I came *for* help you," don't just correct them — thank them\. They just revealed how to build sentences in Spanish\.
