🇪🇸 *Lesson 58: The Past Tense That Changes Everything*

You've been building with one past tense\. Now there's another one — and it works completely differently\.

This is the "point in the past" — like English *\-ed*: walked, talked, bought\. But here's the thing: it's way more random than anything you've learned so far\. The good news? We can still find patterns\.

Let's start with *hablar* \(to speak\):

— *¿Hablaste con ella?*
— *No hablé\. Olvidé su número\.*
— Did you speak with her?
— I didn't speak\. I forgot her number\.

See that? *Hablé* \(I spoke\), *hablaste* \(you spoke\), *olvidé* \(I forgot\)\.

For \-AR verbs, drop the \-ar and add *\-é* for "I" or *\-ó* for "he/she/you formal\." The accent matters — it's what makes it past tense\.

Here are 8 you'll use constantly:

_Hablé_ — I spoke
_Compré_ — I bought
_Envié_ — I sent
_Olvidé_ — I forgot
_Trabajé_ — I worked
_Pagué_ — I paid
_Escuché_ — I listened
_Llamé_ — I called

And for he/she/you formal:

_Habló_ — spoke
_Compró_ — bought
_Envió_ — sent
_Olvidó_ — forgot

💡 When you want to say "it was sent yesterday," don't overthink it\. Just say: *Se envió ayer* \(it sent itself yesterday\)\. No need for extra words\.
