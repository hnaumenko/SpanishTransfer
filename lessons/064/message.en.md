🇪🇸 *Lesson 64*

Ever notice how Spanish skips possessive words way more than English? Where we say "I responded to *her* letter," Spanish just says "I responded her the letter\." Sounds weird in English\. Perfectly natural in Spanish\.

Here's the pattern: Spanish uses *le* \(to him/her\) \+ *el/la* \(the\) instead of "his/her\."

— _¿Le respondiste?_
— Sí, *le respondí el mensaje*\. Hace un mes\.
— Did you respond to her?
— Yes, I responded to her the message\. A month ago\.

Notice *hace un mes* \(it makes one month\) = one month ago\. Spanish literally says "it makes two weeks" instead of "two weeks ago\."

Here are 8 real examples you'll actually use:

_Le respondí_ — I responded to her/him
_Hace dos semanas_ — Two weeks ago
_Le respondí hace un mes_ — I responded to her one month ago
_Le respondí el email_ — I responded to her the email
_¿No le respondiste?_ — Didn't you respond to him?
_¿Dónde viviste?_ — Where did you live?
_¿Por qué no me esperaste?_ — Why didn't you wait for me?
_¿Cuándo llegaste?_ — When did you arrive?

💡 When you know the context, drop "the letter" and just say *le respondí* \(I responded to her it\)\. Sounds broken in English, flows perfectly in Spanish\.
