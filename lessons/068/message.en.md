🇪🇸 *Lesson 68*

Ever noticed how "this" and "that" dance around gender in Spanish? Here's the pattern that unlocks them all\.

The magic starts with *este* \(this\) and *ese* \(that\)\. But here's what blows most learners' minds: sometimes you need a version that has NO gender at all\.

Picture this:
*— I can't believe this\!*
*— ¿Qué es?*
*— Esto\. This price\. This situation\.*

See that? *Esto* \(not *este*\) — because you're not pointing at a masculine noun\. You're pointing at\.\.\. life\.

Here are all the forms you need:

_este hombre_ — this man
_ese hombre_ — that man
_esta noche_ — this night
_esa noche_ — that night
_estos hombres_ — these men
_esos hombres_ — those men
_estas noches_ — these nights
_esas noches_ — those nights

And when there's no specific noun?
*Esto* and *eso*\.

_No creo esto_ — I don't believe this
_Nada me gustó más que eso_ — Nothing pleased me more than that

💡 The "no gender" versions \(*esto*, *eso*\) are your secret weapon when you're talking about situations, ideas, or just\.\.\. stuff\.

👉 *Try it now:* Say "I don't like this" without pointing at anything specific\. Then say "those nights were incredible\."
