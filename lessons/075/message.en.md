🇪🇸 *Lesson 75: Turn "where" into "wherever" and "what" into "whatever"*

Want to sound more flexible in Spanish? There's a mood that transforms ordinary words into their "ever" versions\. Where becomes *wherever*\. What becomes *whatever*\. How becomes *however*\. It's all about the subjunctive mood\.

Here's the magic:
*donde* \(where\) → *donde quieras* \(wherever you want\)
*qué* \(what\) → *lo que quieras* \(whatever you want\)
*como* \(how\) → *como quieras* \(however you like\)

Let's see it in action:
— *Hazlo donde quieras\.*
— But where exactly?
— *Donde quieras\.* Wherever\. I don't mind\.

Now here are 8 ways to use this pattern:

_Hazlo como quieras_ — Do it however you like
_Come lo que prefieras_ — Eat whatever you prefer
_Te sigo adonde vayas_ — I follow you wherever you go
_Cuando necesites_ — Whenever you need
_Lo que sea_ — Whatever it is / whatever will be
_Donde estés_ — Wherever you are
_Como te guste_ — However you like it
_Adonde vayas, voy contigo_ — Wherever you go, I go with you

💡 One quick note: *haz* \(do it\) is irregular\. Most verbs just drop the *\-s* from the *tú* form for commands, but *hacer* gives you *haz*\. So: *hazlo* \(do it\), not *haces\-lo*\.

👉 Try saying it out loud: *Hazlo como quieras* \(Do it however you like\)\.
