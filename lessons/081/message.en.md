🇪🇸 *Lesson 81: The Past Subjunctive — Easier Than You Think*

You already know how to build the past subjunctive\. Here's the secret: find the *they* form in the preterite past, change the final *\-on* to *\-a*, and you're done\.

Watch this:
They spoke — *hablaron*
Past subjunctive — *hablara* \(he/she/it spoke\)

That's it\. No new vocabulary\. Just a quick detour through the preterite *they* form\.

*— Why didn't he call?*
*— Yo quería que él me llamara\.*
\(I wanted that he call me\.\)

In English we say "I wanted him *to call*\." In Spanish: "I wanted that he *called*" — using the past subjunctive\.

Here are 8 examples to lock this in:

_hablara_ — he/she spoke \(subjunctive\)
_comiera_ — he/she ate \(subjunctive\)
_volviera a pasar_ — it happen again \(subjunctive\)
_necesitáramos_ — we needed \(subjunctive\)
_cerrara_ — it close \(subjunctive\)
_organizáramos_ — we organize \(subjunctive\)
_llamara_ — he/she call \(subjunctive\)
_regresara_ — he/she return \(subjunctive\)

💡 Pro move: You can often *avoid* the subjunctive by using an infinitive instead\. Compare:
*Llamé para que organizáramos* — I called so that we'd organize \(subjunctive\)
*Llamé para organizar* — I called in order to organize \(infinitive, easier\!\)

👉 Say this now: *Yo quería que él me llamara otra vez\.* \(I wanted him to call me again\.\)
