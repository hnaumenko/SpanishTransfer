🇪🇸 *Lesson 86: The Go\-Verb Plot Twist*

You know those *go verbs* that act weird in present tense? They're about to get even weirder\.

Here's the pattern: most verbs that add *go* in the "I" form \(like *tengo*, *vengo*, *pongo*\) don't just build their future tenses normally\. They contract\. They drop the *e* and add a *d*\.

Look:
• *tener* → *tendré* \(not *teneré*\)
• *venir* → *vendré* \(not *veniré*\)
• *poner* → *pondré* \(not *poneré*\)

Now imagine this conversation:
*— Will you come later?*
*— Vendré más tarde, but I don't have time\.*
*— We would come, but we're busy\.*
*— Vendríamos, pero estamos ocupados\.*

Here are 8 examples you'll actually use:

_Tendré tiempo_ — I will have time
_Vendría más tarde_ — I would come later
_Pondré eso aquí_ — I will put that here
_Saldría esta noche_ — I would go out tonight
_Tendremos que esperar_ — We will have to wait
_Vendrían pero están ocupados_ — They would come but they're busy
_Pondrías eso aquí_ — You would put that there
_Saldré mañana_ — I will leave tomorrow

💡 *hacer* and *decir* are rebels\. They don't follow this pattern\. They go full irregular: *haré* \(I will do\), *diré* \(I will say\)\.
